# Nichols Photo Lab — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Wide range: from first-timers dropping off their first roll of film to experienced regulars who know exactly what they want. Beginners need clarity and confidence; regulars need efficiency and trust reinforcement. Both groups visit in-person or on mobile, often on the go. The interface should never feel intimidating or overly technical, but should feel credible enough for professionals.

### Brand Personality
Established (45+ years), analog craft, Salt Lake City institution. The brand is not trendy — it's earned. Trustworthy, direct, tactile. The red accent is the only flourish; everything else earns its place. Not a hip Brooklyn coffee shop aesthetic. Not clinical or sterile. Somewhere between a well-worn darkroom and a clean editorial spread.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode only. Cream paper white (`#f9f8f4`) background, near-black (`#010101`) type, `#ff0100` as the single bold accent.
- **Typography**: Inter Variable — bold weights for impact (700–900), regular for body. Large uppercase wordmarks. Tight letter-spacing on display text. No decorative fonts needed; weight and size do the work.
- **Layout**: Editorial. Generous whitespace between sections, tight groupings within sections. Large type as the primary visual element — not imagery.
- **Motion**: Purposeful only. Lenis smooth scroll site-wide. Reveal-arrow on hover for links. No gratuitous animations.
- **Anti-references**: No glassmorphism, no dark mode glow aesthetic, no gradient text, no rounded card grids with icons, no startup-y feel.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Beginners must instantly understand what to do. Reduce friction at every step.
2. **Type does the heavy lifting** — Bold, large typography is the primary design element. Imagery supports it.
3. **Earn every pixel** — No decoration that doesn't serve a function. The red accent appears only where it matters most.
4. **Mobile is primary** — Many customers are on the go, dropping off or picking up film. Mobile experience must be as considered as desktop.
5. **Trust through restraint** — 45+ years of experience speaks for itself. The design reflects that confidence — no overselling, no flashiness.
